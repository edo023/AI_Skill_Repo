# Understanding Mechanical Calculation Results

## Overview

The term *mechanical calculation* in this manual refers to calculating the forces exerted on a mechanical shaft by respecting the following physical quantities:

* Speed
* Torque
* Inertia

These three quantities are key values in determining the suitable size of drive, motor, and gearbox for a given axis. For an introduction to this, refer to the paragraph [*Detailed Information on Step 6, Selecting A Suitable Combination of Drive, Motor, And Gearbox*](D-SE-0058889.html#D-SE-0058889__D-SE-0058889.2).

Motion Sizer displays the results of the mechanical calculations:

* In the Device Selection  dialog box.
* In the Results editor, including the result diagram editors (Torque over Time, Torque over Speed, and  Torque over Gearbox factor).
* In the [messages created by these editors](D-SE-0058721.html#D-SE-0058721).

This chapter provides an introduction to the terminology used in these parts of Motion Sizer.
