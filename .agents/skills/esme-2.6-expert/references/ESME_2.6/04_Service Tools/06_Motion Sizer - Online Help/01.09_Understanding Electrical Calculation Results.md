# Understanding Electrical Calculation Results

## Overview

The term *electrical calculation* in this manual refers to calculations of the DC bus and of the mains supply of drives. These calculations are performed for the drives of multiple axes.

Different drive families allow different electrical architectures. For details, refer to the hardware manuals of the drives you are using.

The  Power Circuits editor of Motion Sizer calculates certain electrical characteristics for the machine you describe.

NOTE: The calculation results are provided only for the hardware selected in Motion Sizer and only apply to the application requirements modeled in Motion Sizer. The calculations assume a repeating, cyclic operation of the motion profiles given for each axis. Due to this assumption, Motion Sizer can only calculate results for power circuits where the drives have motion profiles with the same cycle time. In case of different cycle times, the message [MS2002](D-SE-0058746.html#D-SE-0058746) is generated.

For calculating the effects of regenerative braking on a shared DC bus, the time relation between motion profiles has to be considered. By default, Motion Sizer assumes that the motion diagrams start and end at the same time. You can shift motion diagrams relative to each other by setting the parameter X offset in the properties of a [cam diagram](D-SE-0058886.html#D-SE-0058886__D-SE-0058886.3).

This chapter provides an introduction to the terminology used in Motion Sizer for electrical calculations.
