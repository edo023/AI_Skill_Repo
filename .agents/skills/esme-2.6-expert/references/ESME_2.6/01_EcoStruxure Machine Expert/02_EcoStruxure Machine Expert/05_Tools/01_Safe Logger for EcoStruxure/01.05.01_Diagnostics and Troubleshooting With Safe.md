# Diagnostics and Troubleshooting With Safe Logger

## Diagnostics and Troubleshooting

Inappropriate parameter values, settings or data, incorrect hardware or wiring may trigger unintended movements, trigger unintended or unanticipated signals, disable safety-related functions and monitoring functions, alter the defined safe state and potentially damage equipment.

Diagnosing and trouble-shooting a safety-related application requires comprehensive expertise in the area of safety-related engineering and of functional safety, and a thorough understanding of the safety-related applications and non-safety-related applications used to operate your machine/process.

Safe Logger lets you manage the messages generated by the connected safety-related equipment. The messages allow you to monitor the status of your machine, installation or process, and support you in troubleshooting.

The messages originate from the openSAFETY safety-related nodes connected to the Safety Logic Controller (SLC) of your machine via the Sercos fieldbus and managed by EcoStruxure Machine Expert - Safety.

If a safety-related device detects an error and the error is indicated via Safe Logger, the cause of the error may be the result of a condition in a non-safety-related application on your machine/process. For example, if you increase the load to be moved by means of an axis of a Lexium 62 drive, the values of the control loop parameters for the Lexium 62 drive may no longer be adequate. This may cause a safety-related function to trigger. In this example, you would have to tune the control loop parameters in a non-safety-related application. Modifying parameter values in the safety-related application would not remedy the condition.
