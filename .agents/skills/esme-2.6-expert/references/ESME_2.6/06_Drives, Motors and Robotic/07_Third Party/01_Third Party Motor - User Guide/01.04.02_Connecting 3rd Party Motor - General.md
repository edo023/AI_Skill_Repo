Connecting 3rd Party Motor - General Information

General Information

Overview

If other motors (of any design) are connected to the Lexium 52 / Lexium 62 besides the Lexium SH3 / Lexium MH3 / Lexium SHS stainless steel, then the following notes and conditions must be observed:

|  |
| --- |
| Warning_Color.gifWARNING |
| ELECTRICAL HAZARD DUE TO INSUFFICIENT ELECTRICAL STRENGTH |
| The motors must fulfil the overvoltage category III according to the IEC 61800-5-1 standard, table 7 (English Edition 07/2007). |
| Failure to follow these instructions can result in death, serious injury, or equipment damage. |

oThe insulation must be designed for at least 1000 V.

oThe dielectric strength is also required for single phase supply of the DC link.

oThe standstill current should be in the range between 30 % and 100 % of the rated current of the Lexium servo amplifier used.

oTo ensure thermal monitoring, the motor should be equipped with a temperature sensor. Basically the common electrical resistor elements, whose electrical resistance increases with increasing temperature, can be used as temperature sensors (for example, KTY sensors, PTC sensors or bimetal switches).

oThe motor can optionally be equipped with a brake if it is supplied with 24 V and the specifications listed in the operating manual of the servo amplifier are fulfilled.

oEspecially the maximum current consumption must be considered.

oSupported are linear synchronous motors with permanent excitation that are designed for operation with three-phase, sinusoidal supply.

oSupported are asynchronous motors by cage rotors.

oAn operation with a delta connection of the motor phases is only for asynchronous motor possible.

|  |
| --- |
| NOTICE |
| STAR-DELTA CONNECTION |
| A star-delta connection in the operation is not permitted. |
| Failure to follow these instructions can result in equipment damage. |

By using an appropriate surge protection device, that is integrated into the network supply the necessary overvoltage category III can be reduced to II. For questions on this, contact your Schneider Electric representative.

The motor also has to fulfill the requirements for the converter operation according to IEC TS 60034-25, Edition 03/2007. Especially the barrier (Phase - Phase and Phase - Ground) must fulfill the minimum requirements according to Figure 14, curve A, IEC TS 60034-25 Edition 03/2007. Nonobservance can cause motor damage or reduce the lifetime of the motor drastically.

NOTE: Rises of voltage up to 8 kV/μs can occur at the output of Lexium 52 / Lexium 62. In order to prevent a reduction of the service life, it must be checked whether the winding of the motor is suitable for the required rise of voltage.
