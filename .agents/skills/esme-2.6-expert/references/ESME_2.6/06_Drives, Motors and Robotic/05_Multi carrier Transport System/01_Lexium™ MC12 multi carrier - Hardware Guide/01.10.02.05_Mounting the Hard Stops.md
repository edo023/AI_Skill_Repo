# Mounting the Hard Stops

## General Information

With an open track, the carriers could leave the track at the ends. Therefore, mechanical hard stops must be mounted at both ends of an open track.

| WARNING | |
| --- | --- |
|  | Unintended Equipment OPERATION  Mount mechanical hard stops at both ends of an open track.  Failure to follow these instructions can result in death, serious injury, or equipment damage. |

## Mounting

Mounting of the hard stops is carried out similarly to the mounting of the track ([Mounting the Lexium™ MC12 multi carrier](MountingThe-5FAA5905.html#MountingThe-5FAA5905)).

**Horizontal Mounting**

| Step | Action |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | After mounting the power interconnects (**a**) and the segments (**b**), insert a left hard stop (**c**) from above into the left power interconnect and a right hard stop (**d**) into the right power interconnect. |
| 2 | Fasten the hard stops loosely with two screws (M6x120 class 8.8 ISO 4762) to the mounting plate so that they stay in place but can still be shifted a little. |
| 3 | Then proceed with the installation of the guide rails as described in [Horizontal Mounting of the Track without Automated Lubrication](HorizontalMounting-C03CA1A8.html) or [Horizontal Mounting of the Track with Automated Lubrication](HorizMountingWithAutomatedLubr-C03D6E55.html). NOTE: Additionally, for the guide rail mounting procedures specific to the Heavy-Duty system, refer to [Mounting a Lexium™ MC12 Heavy-Duty Guide Rail without Automated Lubrication](MountingHDGuideRail-F8EE6A79.html) and [Mounting a Lexium™ MC12 Heavy-Duty Guide Rail with Automated Lubrication](MountingHDGuideRailAutLub-D89DDB43.html). |

**Vertical Mounting**

| Step | Action |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | After mounting the power interconnects (**a**) and the segments (**b**), insert a left hard stop (**c**) from above into the left power interconnect and a right hard stop (**d**) into the right power interconnect. |
| 2 | Fasten the hard stops loosely with two screws (M6x120 class 8.8 ISO 4762) to the mounting plate so that they stay in place but can still be shifted a little. |
| 3 | Then proceed with the installation of the guide rails as described in [Vertical Mounting of the Track without Automated Lubrication](VerticalMounting-C03D5C93.html) or [Vertical Mounting of the Track with Automated Lubrication](VerticalMountingWithAutomatedLubric-C03EFBED.html). NOTE: Additionally, for the guide rail mounting procedures specific to the Heavy-Duty system, refer to [Mounting a Lexium™ MC12 Heavy-Duty Guide Rail without Automated Lubrication](MountingHDGuideRail-F8EE6A79.html) and [Mounting a Lexium™ MC12 Heavy-Duty Guide Rail with Automated Lubrication](MountingHDGuideRailAutLub-D89DDB43.html). |
