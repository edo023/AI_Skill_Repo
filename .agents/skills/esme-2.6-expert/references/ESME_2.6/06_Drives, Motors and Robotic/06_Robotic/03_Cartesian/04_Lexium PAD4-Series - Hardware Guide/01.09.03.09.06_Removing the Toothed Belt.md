# Removing the Toothed Belt

Removing the Toothed Belt

To remove the toothed belt, perform the tasks described below at both ends of the carriage and at both end blocks.

NOTE: If you have already removed the rubber buffers and the strip deflectors due to removing the cover strip, proceed with step 4.

| Step | Action |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | Remove the two screws (3) at the rubber buffer (2).  G-SE-0083050.1.gif-high.gif |
| 2 | Remove the rubber buffer from the holding plate (1). |
| 3 | Remove the two screws (4) and the holding plate from the carriage.  G-SE-0083051.1.gif-high.gif |
| 4 | Perform this step only when using distance measurement:  Measure the position of the toothed belt tensioner (5) with a caliper gauge at both sides of the carriage and note the values. The distance e has to be measured from the front side of the toothed belt tensioner to the front side of the carriage.  G-SE-0083052.1.gif-high.gif |
| 5 | Remove the tensioning screw (6) at the toothed belt tensioner. |
| 6 | Pull out the toothed belt tensioner along with the toothed belt (7).  NOTE: This may require a considerable force.  G-SE-0083053.1.gif-high.gif |
| 7 | Loosen the two setscrews (9) at the toothed belt tensioner.  NOTE: This requires a considerable force due to the threadlocker.  G-SE-0083054.1.gif-high.gif |
| 8 | Slide the toothed belt and the clamping plate (8) to the side out of the toothed belt tensioner. |
| 9 | Remove the four screws (11) at the cover of the end block (10).  G-SE-0083055.1.gif-high.gif |
| 10 | Remove the cover. |
| 11 | Remove the toothed belt.  G-SE-0083056.1.gif-high.gif |
