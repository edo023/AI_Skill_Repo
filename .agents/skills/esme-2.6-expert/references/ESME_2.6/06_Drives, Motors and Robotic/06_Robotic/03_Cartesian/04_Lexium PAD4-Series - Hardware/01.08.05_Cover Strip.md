# Cover Strip

![](images/logo.png)

Cover Strip

Mounting the Cover Strip

Overview

The edges of the cover strip are sharp.

|  |
| --- |
| Warning_Color.gifWARNING |
| SHARP EDGES |
| Wear protective gloves when cutting and installing the cover strip. |
| Failure to follow these instructions can result in death, serious injury, or equipment damage. |

NOTE: When the cover strip is mounted, the stroke of the axis decreases.

Parts for Cover Strip Mounting

A set of parts is required to mount the cover strip.

The following graphic presents the components of this set:

![G-SE-0082864.1.gif-high.gif](images/G-SE-0082864.1.gif-high.gif)

1   Cover strip (2 pieces)

2   Cover strip clamp (4 pieces)

3   Hex socket screw (8 pieces)

4   Strip deflector (4 pieces)

5   Magnetic strip (4 pieces)

Prerequisites

You need the following tools to mount the cover strip:

oScissors

oTorque wrench with a set of hexagon sockets

oMetal shears

NOTE: Do not use ball head hex keys. Excessive torque may cause the ball head to break away.

For suitable parts, refer to [Replacement Equipment and Accessories](../ROBOTICS_Replacement_Equipment/ROBOTICS_Replacement_Equipment-1.htm#XREF_D_SE_0065517_1).

Perform the following procedures at both ends of the carriages and at all end blocks for axis types PAD42BB and PAD42EB or at all end plates for axis type PAD42PB to mount the cover strip:

Preparing the Mounting of the Cover Strip

| Step | Action |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | Clean all parts. |
| 2 | Inspect all parts for damage. |
| 3 | Remove the motor or the gearbox as described under [Replacing the Motor or Gearbox](../ROBOTICS_Maintenance_and_Repair/ROBOTICS_Maintenance_and_Repair-14.htm#XREF_D_SE_0106422_1) so that you can easily move the carriage. |

NOTE: Polluted or damaged parts may cause run-out which has an adverse effect on the service life of the axis.

Fastening the Magnetic Strips

| Step | Action |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | Clean the mounting surfaces of the magnetic strips at the axis body (1) such that they are free of grease.  G-SE-0082855.1.gif-high.gif |
| 2 | Manually move the carriages into end position on the right-hand side. |
| 3 | Place the magnetic strips flush with the face of the mounting surface along the axis body from the end blocks on the left-hand side to the carriages on the right-hand side and cut them with the scissors. |
| 4 | Peel off the film of the magnetic strips (2) inch by inch and fasten them with the glued side onto the mounting surface of the axis body.  G-SE-0082856.1.gif-high.gif      NOTE:  oDo not pull. Lay down the magnetic strip inch by inch on the mounting surface and then press it down.  oAllow the adhesive to cure for 24 hours before starting operation. |
| 5 | Manually move the carriages to the left-hand side until you have enough space on the right-hand side to fasten the magnetic strips to the rest of the mounting surfaces. |
| 6 | Place the magnetic strips flush with the face of the mounting surface along the axis body from the end of the already fastened magnetic strips to the end blocks on the right-hand side and cut them with the scissors. |
| 7 | Peel off the film of the magnetic strips (2) inch by inch and fasten them with the glued side onto the mounting surface of the axis body. |

Mounting the Cover Strip

| Step | Action |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | Guide the cover strip (1) through the guide channel inside the carriage (2).  G-SE-0082857.1.gif-high.gif      NOTE: Wear protective gloves. |
| 2 | Guide the strip deflector (3) over the cover strip to the carriage. Take care to use the correct side of the strip deflector.  G-SE-0082858.1.gif-high.gif |
| 3 | Fasten the strip deflector and the holding plate (4) to the carriage with the two screws (5) at both sides of the carriage. Use the [standard tightening torque](../ROBOTICS_Transport_and_Comissioning/ROBOTICS_Transport_and_Comissioning-6.htm#XREF_D_SE_0088555_4). Verify that there is no clearance between the carriage, the strip deflector, and the holding plate such that the surfaces are flush.  G-SE-0082859.1.gif-high.gif |
| 4 | Place the cover strip from one of the two end blocks at a distance of 1…2 mm (0.04…0.08 in). Verify that there is no clearance between the cover strip and the magnetic strips such that the surfaces are flush.  G-SE-0082860.1.gif-high.gif |
| 5 | Fit the clamping plate (6) into place.  G-SE-0082861.1.gif-high.gif |
| 6 | Fasten the cover strip clamp (7) to the end block with the two screws (8). Verify that there is no clearance between the cover strip clamp and the end block such that the surfaces are flush.  Tightening torque: 1 Nm (8.9 lbf-in) |
| 7 | Tighten the two setscrews (9) of the cover strip clamp.  Tightening torque: 0.3 Nm (0.0118 lbf-in)  NOTE: Tighten the screws alternately so that the cover strip does not curve. If the cover strip curves, loosen and retighten the screws.  G-SE-0082862.1.gif-high.gif |
| 8 | Cut the cover strip at the other end with the metal shear to length such that the distance between the cover strip and the second end block is also 1…2 mm (0.04…0.08 in). |
| 9 | Repeat steps 5, 6 and 7 for the end block on the other side. Pull the cover strip before tightening it to avoid curving. |
| 10 | Fasten the rubber buffer (10) to the holding plate at the carriage with the two screws (11). Use the [standard tightening torque](../ROBOTICS_Transport_and_Comissioning/ROBOTICS_Transport_and_Comissioning-6.htm#XREF_D_SE_0088555_4). Verify that there is no clearance between the rubber buffer and the holding plate such that the surfaces are flush.  G-SE-0082863.1.gif-high.gif |
