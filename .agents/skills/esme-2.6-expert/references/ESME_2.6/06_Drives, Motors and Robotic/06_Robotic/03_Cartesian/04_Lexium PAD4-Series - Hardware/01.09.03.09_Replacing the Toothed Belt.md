# Replacing the Toothed Belt

![](images/logo.png)

Replacing the Toothed Belt

Overview

Replacing the toothed belt can cause unintended movements.

|  |
| --- |
| Warning_Color.gifWARNING |
| UNINTENDED MOVEMENTS DUE TO DISMOUNTING |
| Secure the moving parts of the axis mounted in a vertical or tilted position against unintended movements. |
| Failure to follow these instructions can result in death, serious injury, or equipment damage. |

Prerequisites

You need the following tools to remove the toothed belt:

oSet of hex keys

oMedium strength threadlocker

oTorque wrench with a set of hexagon sockets

NOTE: Do not use ball head hex keys. Excessive torque may cause the ball head to break away.

To adjust the toothed belt tension, you need a:

oCaliper gauge (for distance measurement) or

oBelt tension meter (for vibration measurement)

For suitable parts, refer to [Replacement Equipment and Accessories](../ROBOTICS_Replacement_Equipment/ROBOTICS_Replacement_Equipment-1.htm#XREF_D_SE_0065517_1).

Distance and Vibration Measurement

For adjusting the toothed belt tension, you can use either distance measurement or vibration measurement:

oDistance measurement

The position of the toothed belt tensioner is measured with a caliper gauge. The position of the toothed belt tensioner is used to preload the toothed belt.

oVibration measurement

To restore the precise factory-adjusted toothed belt tension, use a belt tension meter for vibration measurement. The factory-adjusted toothed belt tension is presented in the following table. The measured preload values FV depend on the selectable measuring distance A and the weight of the respective toothed belt.

The measuring distance A is measured:

oFrom the center of the end block

oTo the edge of the carriage

![G-SE-0083049.1.gif-high.gif](images/G-SE-0083049.1.gif-high.gif)

| Description | Parameter | Unit | Value |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| PAD42 |
| Toothed belt type | – | – | HTD5 |
| Width | – | mm (in) | 25 (0.98) |
| Pitch | – | 5 (0.197) |
| Weight | – | g/m (lb/ft) | 96 (0.065) |
| Toothed belt tension | Fv | N (lbf) | 570…710  (128…160) |

Preparing the Removal of the Toothed Belt

| Step | Action |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | If the axis is mounted tilted or vertically, remove the payload or support the payload and the carriages to keep it from falling. |
| 2 | Remove the following components depending on the axis configuration:  o[Motor and/or gearbox](ROBOTICS_Maintenance_and_Repair-14.htm#XREF_D_SE_0106422_1)  o[Coupling](ROBOTICS_Maintenance_and_Repair-15.htm#XREF_D_SE_0106423_1)  o[Double coupling (only PAD42BB)](ROBOTICS_Maintenance_and_Repair-20.htm#XREF_D_SE_0106084_1)  o[Cover strip](ROBOTICS_Maintenance_and_Repair-17.htm#XREF_D_SE_0106097_1) |

Removing the Toothed Belt

To remove the toothed belt, perform the tasks described below at both ends of the carriage and at both end blocks.

NOTE: If you have already removed the rubber buffers and the strip deflectors due to removing the cover strip, proceed with step 4.

| Step | Action |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | Remove the two screws (3) at the rubber buffer (2).  G-SE-0083050.1.gif-high.gif |
| 2 | Remove the rubber buffer from the holding plate (1). |
| 3 | Remove the two screws (4) and the holding plate from the carriage.  G-SE-0083051.1.gif-high.gif |
| 4 | Perform this step only when using distance measurement:  Measure the position of the toothed belt tensioner (5) with a caliper gauge at both sides of the carriage and note the values. The distance e has to be measured from the front side of the toothed belt tensioner to the front side of the carriage.  G-SE-0083052.1.gif-high.gif |
| 5 | Remove the tensioning screw (6) at the toothed belt tensioner. |
| 6 | Pull out the toothed belt tensioner along with the toothed belt (7).  NOTE: This may require a considerable force.  G-SE-0083053.1.gif-high.gif |
| 7 | Loosen the two setscrews (9) at the toothed belt tensioner.  NOTE: This requires a considerable force due to the threadlocker.  G-SE-0083054.1.gif-high.gif |
| 8 | Slide the toothed belt and the clamping plate (8) to the side out of the toothed belt tensioner. |
| 9 | Remove the four screws (11) at the cover of the end block (10).  G-SE-0083055.1.gif-high.gif |
| 10 | Remove the cover. |
| 11 | Remove the toothed belt.  G-SE-0083056.1.gif-high.gif |

Cutting the New Toothed Belt to Length

| Step | Action |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | Place the new toothed belt (1) and the previous toothed belt (2) next to each other. Align the teeth with each other.  G-SE-0057211.3.gif-high.gif |
| 2 | Cut the new toothed belt to the same length as the previous toothed belt.  G-SE-0057212.2.gif-high.gif      NOTE: The number of teeth must be the same. |

Mounting the Toothed Belt

| Step | Action |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | Insert one end of the new toothed belt (3) with up-pointing teeth into an end block below the toothed belt pulley (2). Slide the toothed belt through the axis body (1) until the toothed belt comes out on the other end of the axis.  G-SE-0083017.1.gif-high.gif |
| 2 | Insert the other end of the toothed belt into the end block above the toothed belt pulley.  G-SE-0083018.1.gif-high.gif |
| 3 | Insert the end of the toothed belt from the side into the toothed belt tensioner (6) so that all five teeth in the toothed belt tensioner are covered.  G-SE-0083019.1.gif-high.gif |
| 4 | Insert the clamping plate (4) with the recess pointing upward from the side into the toothed belt tensioner.  NOTE: Verify that the toothed belt is inserted symmetrically and the clamping plate is inserted flush into the toothed belt tensioner. |
| 5 | Apply a thin layer of medium strength threadlocker to the setscrews (5) at the toothed belt tensioner and tighten the setscrews.  Tightening torque: 0.15 Nm (0.0059 lbf-in): |
| 6 | Insert the toothed belt tensioner with the mounted toothed belt into the carriage (7).  G-SE-0083020.1.gif-high.gif |
| 7 | Repeat steps 2, 3, 4, 5, and 6 on the other side of the carriage. |
| 8 | Tension the toothed belt by adjusting the tensioning screws (8) at both ends of the carriage. Use either distance or vibration measurement:  oWith distance measurement: tension the tensioning screw with the values noted in [Removing the Toothed Belt](#XREF_D_SE_0106425_10) at both sides of the carriage.  oWith vibration measurement: tension the tensioning screw until the respective toothed belt tension is reached.  G-SE-0083021.1.gif-high.gif      NOTE: For the PAD42BB the double coupling has to be unlocked before tensioning the toothed belt. For further information on unlocking the double coupling, refer to [Replacing the Double Coupling](ROBOTICS_Maintenance_and_Repair-20.htm#XREF_D_SE_0106084_1). |
| 9 | If applicable, mount the strip deflectors and the cover strip. For further information, refer to [Mounting the Cover Strip](../ROBOTICS_Optional_Equipment/ROBOTICS_Optional_Equipment-9.htm#XREF_D_SE_0106254_1). |
| 10 | Fasten the holding plate (9) to the carriage with the two screws (10). Verify that there is no clearance between the holding plate and the carriage such that the surfaces are flush.  G-SE-0083022.1.gif-high.gif |
| 11 | Fasten the rubber buffer (11) to the holding plate with the two screws (12). Verify that there is no clearance between the rubber buffer and the holding plate such that the surfaces are flush.  G-SE-0083023.1.gif-high.gif |
| 12 | Repeat steps 8, 9, 10, and 11 on the other side of the carriage. |

Testing Movements

| Step | Action |
| --- | --- |

| 3 | Mount the payload. |
