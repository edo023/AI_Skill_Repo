# Troubleshooting

## Overview

| Issue | Probable cause | Solution |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Sensor over-travel | Detected sensor error | The sensors must be adjusted or replaced, contact your local Schneider Electric service representative. |
| Detected controller error | Verify the controller. |
| Motor load increases, controller switches off because of overload | Linear guides or toothed belt pulleys under mechanical tension or excessive friction caused by poor lubrication | Contact your local Schneider Electric service representative. |
| Noise and vibrations | Velocity too high | Reduce velocity. |
| Poor lubrication (in the case of noise) | [Lubricate the axis](D-SE-0057273.html). |
| Carriage has backlash | Contact your local Schneider Electric service representative. |
| Ambient conditions | Verify [ambient conditions](AmbientConditions-623F1CD9.html). |
| Ball bearings are worn out or have backlash | Verify the ball bearings. |
| Toothed belt tension | Verify the toothed belt tension and, if necessary, use PTFE-spray on the toothed belt. For further information about the toothed belt tension, contact your local Schneider Electric service representative. |
| Running inaccuracy and noise of the guides | Poor lubrication | [Lubricate the axis](D-SE-0057273.html). |
| Damage to components of the linear guide, for example by shock or impact on the carriage | The components of the linear guide must be replaced, contact your local Schneider Electric service representative. |
| The carriage has backlash and positions inaccurately | Backlash in guides after a collision | Contact your local Schneider Electric service representative. |
| Worn out or damaged toothed belt or coupling | The toothed belt and/or the coupling must be replaced, contact your local Schneider Electric service representative. |
