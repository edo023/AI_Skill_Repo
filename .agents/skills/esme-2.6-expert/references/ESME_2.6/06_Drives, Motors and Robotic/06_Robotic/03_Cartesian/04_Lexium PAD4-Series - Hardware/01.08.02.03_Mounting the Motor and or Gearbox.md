# Mounting the Motor and/or Gearbox

![](images/logo.png)

Mounting the Motor and/or Gearbox

Overview

Optionally, the axis is supplied with a preinstalled Schneider Electric motor and/or a gearbox.

|  |
| --- |
| NOTICE |
| DISTORTION OF MOTOR AND GEARBOX |
| Only fasten motor and gearbox with all components at the same ambient temperature. |
| Failure to follow these instructions can result in equipment damage. |

Mounting Position of Motor and Gearbox

The correct mounting of the motor and gearbox is depending on the axis type:

oFor PAD42BB: mount the motor and/or gearbox to one of the accessible sides of the end blocks where the double coupling is installed. If needed, the double coupling can also be mounted to the other end of the axis. For further information, refer to [Replacing the Double Coupling](../ROBOTICS_Maintenance_and_Repair/ROBOTICS_Maintenance_and_Repair-20.htm#XREF_D_SE_0106084_1)

oFor PAD42EB: you can mount motors and/or gearboxes to either accessible side of the end blocks.

oFor PAD42PB: you can mount the motor and/or gearbox to either side of the end block where the distance plate is installed.

The motor and the gearbox can be mounted in different arrangements (turned in increments of 4 x 90°). For further information, refer to [Motor and/or Gearbox Orientation and Configuration](../ROBOTICS_System_Overview/ROBOTICS_System_Overview-4.htm#XREF_D_SE_0104489_4).

NOTE: The maximum mass of the installed parts is limited by the torque at the end block.

The mass of the motor and/or gearbox which can be mounted to the end block is limited.

Maximum Torque

A mounted motor and/or gearbox causes a static overturning torque at the end block. In case of a lateral acceleration of the complete axis, the mounted motor and/or gearbox cause an additional dynamic overturning torque. The total of the static and dynamic overturning torque is limited by the maximum overturning torque of the end block.

![G-SE-0082877.1.gif-high.gif](images/G-SE-0082877.1.gif-high.gif)

T   Torque at the end block

a   Lateral acceleration of the axis

The following table presents the maximum permissible torques of the mounted motor and/or gearbox at the end block:

| Parameter | Unit | Value |
| --- | --- | --- |
| PAD42 |
| Maximum permissible torque (total of static and dynamic) | Nm (lbf-in) | 85 (752) |

NOTE: The total of the static and dynamic torques must not exceed the maximum permissible torque at the end block.

Third-Party Motors and Gearboxes

When choosing a third-party motor, take special care that the maximum drive torque is not exceeded. Otherwise the axis could be damaged or rendered inoperable.

Coupling for Motor and Gearbox Mounting

A jaw elastomer coupling is required to mount a motor or gearbox to the axis.

The following graphic presents the components of a jaw elastomer coupling:

1   Expanding hub (1 piece)

2   Elastomer spider (1 piece)

3   Clamping hub (1 piece)

Prerequisites

You need the following tools to mount the motor and/or the gearbox:

oTorque wrench with a set of hexagon sockets

oCaliper gauge (for distance measurement)

oSoft-faced hammer

NOTE: Do not use ball head hex keys. Excessive torque may cause the ball head to break away.

For suitable parts, refer to [Replacement Equipment and Accessories](../ROBOTICS_Replacement_Equipment/ROBOTICS_Replacement_Equipment-1.htm#XREF_D_SE_0065517_1).

Mounting the Coupling

If possible, position the axis such that the top of the carriages points upwards during installation.

| Step | Action |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | Manually move the carriages (1) into end position on the right-hand side.  G-SE-0082866.1.gif-high.gif |
| 2 | Fit the expanding hub (3) into the hollow shaft of the toothed belt pulley (2). Verify that there is no clearance between the expanding hub and the toothed belt pulley such that the surfaces are flush.  NOTE: To completely fit the expanding hub, you may have to tap the expanding hub slightly with a soft-faced hammer (not on the screw head). |
| 3 | Tighten the screw (4) of the expanding hub.  Tightening torque: 10 Nm (89 lbf-in)  NOTE: The expanding hub shall not rotate further during tightening. If it does, verify that the carriages are at the end position and the toothed belt is installed correctly. |
| 4 | Fit the elastomer spider (5) into the expanding hub.  G-SE-0082867.1.gif-high.gif      NOTE:  oIf the elastomer spider can be fitted too easily (without preloading), it must be replaced.  oTo facilitate the fitting process, grease the elastomer spider slightly. Use only mineral oilbased lubricants without additives or silicon-based lubricants. |
| 5 | Fasten the coupling housing (7) to the end block (6) with the four screws (8). Use the [standard tightening torque](../ROBOTICS_Transport_and_Comissioning/ROBOTICS_Transport_and_Comissioning-6.htm#XREF_D_SE_0088555_4). Verify that there is no clearance between the coupling housing and the end block such that the surfaces are flush.  G-SE-0082868.1.gif-high.gif      NOTE: Greasing every centering collar provides easier dismounting. Use mineral oil-based lubricants without additives, or use silicon-based lubricants. |
| 6 | Insert the clamping hub (9) approximately 5 mm (0.197 in) into the elastomer spider.  G-SE-0082869.1.gif-high.gif |
| 7 | Verify the dimension d of the clamping hub and adjust the clamping hub if necessary. Measure the dimension from the top of the clamping hub to the collar of the coupling housing.  Dimension d: 13 mm (0.51 in)  G-SE-0082870.1.gif-high.gif |

Mounting the Motor Only

If possible, position the axis such that the top of the carriages points upwards during installation.

| Step | Action |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | Remove the setscrew (1) at the drive unit adaptation.  G-SE-0083132.1.gif-high.gif |
| 2 | Verify that the orientation of the clamping screw (2) is the same as the orientation of the setscrew of the drive unit adaptation. |
| 3 | Fit the motor (5) into the clamping hub and to the drive unit adaptation and secure the motor from falling. Verify that there is no clearance between the motor and the drive unit adaptation such that the surfaces are flush.  G-SE-0082878.1.gif-high.gif      NOTE: Greasing every centering collar provides easier dismounting. Use mineral oil-based lubricants without additives, or use silicon-based lubricants. |
| 4 | Fasten the motor with the four screws (3) and washers (4) crosswise. Use the [standard tightening torque](../ROBOTICS_Transport_and_Comissioning/ROBOTICS_Transport_and_Comissioning-6.htm#XREF_D_SE_0088555_4). |
| 5 | Tighten the screw of the clamping hub through the hole of the drive unit adaptation.  Tightening torque: 14 Nm (124 lbf-in) |
| 6 | Insert the setscrew into the drive unit adaptation and tighten it.  Tightening torque: 4 Nm (35.4 lbf-in) |

Mounting the Gearbox Only

A flange plate is required if the gearbox does not have its own flange. If possible, position the axis such that the top of the carriages points upwards during installation.

| Step | Action |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | Fasten the flange plate (2) to the gearbox (3) with the four screws (1). Use the [standard tightening torque](../ROBOTICS_Transport_and_Comissioning/ROBOTICS_Transport_and_Comissioning-6.htm#XREF_D_SE_0088555_4). Verify that there is no clearance between the flange plate and the gearbox such that the surfaces are flush.  G-SE-0082873.1.gif-high.gif      NOTE: Greasing every centering collar provides easier dismounting. Use mineral oil-based lubricants without additives, or use silicon-based lubricants. |
| 2 | Remove the setscrew (4) at the drive unit adaptation.  G-SE-0083133.1.gif-high.gif |
| 3 | Verify that the orientation of the clamping screw (5) is the same as the orientation of the setscrew of the drive unit adaptation. |
| 4 | Fit the flange plate complete with gearbox into the clamping hub (6) and to the drive unit adaptation and secure the gearbox from falling. Verify that there is no clearance between the gearbox and the drive unit adaptation such that the surfaces are flush.  G-SE-0082874.1.gif-high.gif      NOTE: Greasing every centering collar provides easier dismounting. Use mineral oil-based lubricants without additives, or use silicon-based lubricants. |
| 5 | Fasten the flange plate complete with gearbox with the four screws (7). Use the [standard tightening torque](../ROBOTICS_Transport_and_Comissioning/ROBOTICS_Transport_and_Comissioning-6.htm#XREF_D_SE_0088555_4). |
| 6 | Tighten the screw of the clamping hub through the hole in the drive unit adaptation.  Tightening torque: 14 Nm (124 lbf-in) |
| 7 | Insert the setscrew into the drive unit adaptation and tighten the setscrew.  Tightening torque: 4 Nm (35.4 lbf-in) |
