# Mounting the Sensors

![](images/logo.png)

Mounting the Sensors

Overview

The use of sensors can provide some protection against hazards (for example, collision with mechanical stop caused by incorrect reference values).

For the functionality of the inductive sensors, a contact plate must be mounted to one of the carriages for the axis types PAD42BB and PAD42PB or to each carriage for the axis type PAD42EB. Fastening threads are located at both outer sides of the carriages.

NOTE: If possible, use normally closed contacts as sensors so that a wire break can be signaled as an error.

Fastening Material for Sensor Mounting

A sensor holder is required to mount a sensor to the axis body. The axis body provides a T-slot for the sensor holder. This T-slot has cutouts at both end blocks for inserting the fastening nuts. In addition, the sensor holder has cams at both sides to keep the sensor from turning in the T-slot.

The following graphic presents the components of a sensor holder:

![G-SE-0082884.1.gif-high.gif](images/G-SE-0082884.1.gif-high.gif)

1   Fastening nut\* (2 pieces)

2   Sensor holder (1 piece)

3   Hex socket screw (2 pieces)

\* For fastening the sensor in the sensor holder and the sensor holder in the T-Slot.

Mounting the Sensor to the Sensor Holder

Before you mount the sensor, be aware of the switching distance:

NOTE: Since the sensor operates inductively, the sensor surface must have a specific distance from the contact plate. This switching distance is 0.5 +/- 0.1 mm (0.00197 +/- 0.00039 in).

| Step | Action |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | Move the carriages (1) until the contact plate (2) is above the sensor holder (4).  G-SE-0082883.1.gif-high.gif |
| 2 | Slide the sensor (5) through the sensor holder opening until the switching distance s is obtained. Use a feeler gauge to measure the switching distance. |
| 3 | Tighten the screw on the slitted side of the sensor holder (3) to fix the sensor.  Tightening torque: 0.3 Nm (2.66 lbf-in). |
| 4 | Verify the correct switching distance s with the feeler gauge.  G-SE-0083134.1.gif-high.gif |
