# Third-Party Motors

Third-Party Motors

When operating with third-party motors, it must be understood that the drive can only generate a limited electric frequency.

The resulting maximum speed depends on the [PowerStageFrequency](../ControlLoop_2/ControlLoop_2-14.htm#XREF_D_SE_0071575_1) and can be calculated as follows:

| Formula | Condition |
| --- | --- |
| speedmax= 96000 (rpm) / pair of poles | At PowerStageFrequency 8 kHz or 16 kHz |
| speedmax= 48000 (rpm) / pair of poles | At PowerStageFrequency 4 kHz |

If this speed of rotation is exceeded, an impairment of the motor can occur. In the case of multi-pole motors (for example, torque motors), this limit can be reached at relatively low speeds. Motors that can reach a higher maximum speed may only be operated to the limit specified here. The maximum velocity can be limited using the parameter [UserMaxVel](General_2-3.htm#XREF_D_SE_0071525_1).
