# Input Power

The input current of your controller is indicated in the following table and on the nameplate, item Input Device. The value indicated applies to the controller as delivered.

| Controller | Input current |
| --- | --- |
| MN660C153N1010 | 1.4 A |
| MN660C253N1010 | 1.5 A |
| MN660C254N1010 | 2.0 A |
| MN660C354N2010 | 2.4 A |
| MN660C455N2010 | 3.0 A |
| MN660P253N2011W0 | 1.7 A |
| MN660P254N2012W0 | 2.2 A |
| MN660P354N2013W0 | 2.6 A |
| MN660P455N2013W0 | 3.4 A |
| MN660P455N2023W0 | 3.6 A |

If you install additional equipment in the PCI Express Mini Card (MPCIe) slots and/or PCI Express Card (PCIe) slots that your controller may feature (refer to [Type Code](TypeCode-E6EB6EB6.html)), the input power requirements increase. This also applies if you install an optional fan kit (commercial reference MNAH6AFS01) in a controller originally provided without a fan kit.

The following table lists the additional input power required depending on the equipment installed by you:

| Parameter | MPCIe slot 1 | MPCIe slot 2 | PCIe slot 1 | PCIe slot 2 | Fan kit |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Maximum power provided at 3.3 V | 3.6 W | 3.6 W | 8.0 W | 8.0 W | - |
| Maximum power provided at 12.0 V | - | - | 8.0 W | 8.0 W | 2.5 W |
| Maximum increase of input current at 24 V with reference to current specified on the nameplate | 0.17 A | 0.17 A | 0.77 A | 0.77 A | 0.12 A |

NOTE: The increases specified apply without power required by consumers connected to the USB ports and the DisplayPortTM. Corresponding increases depend on the power consumption of the connected equipment. For details on the power provided by the USB ports and the DisplayPortTM, refer to [USB Ports](USBPorts-E70A9E6A.html) and to [DisplayPort](DisplayPort-E70B050A.html).

NOTE: The specified currents relate to the nominal voltage of 24 V. If your power supply unit does not provide the nominal voltage of 24 V, but a different value within the permissible voltage range of -15 % ... +25 % (refer to [Power Supply](PowerSupply-E70A4DF9.html)), the currents decrease or increase proportionally.
