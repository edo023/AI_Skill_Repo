# Sercos Topology

Sercos III supports three network topologies:

* Ring topology
* Line topology
* Double line topology

The Modicon M660 controller supports these three topologies.

Each telegram “begins” and “ends” at the master. This is possible due to full duplex operation.

Each subordinate device has two communication ports. The ports are interchangeable. The telegrams for a cycle originate from the master. They are transmitted to the first subordinate device on the line which receives them through its receive port, reads the MDTs and, if necessary, writes data to the slot reserved to it in the ATs. The first subordinate device then forwards the telegrams through its transmit port to the next subordinate device on the line. This procedure is repeated until the last Sercos device is reached.

In a line, the last subordinate device detects no further Sercos device on the line and redirects the telegrams back to the master (loopback). On their way back to the master, the telegrams pass each subordinate device a second time in reverse order.

The last subordinate device on the line also makes the received telegrams (Sercos and Ethernet/IP) available at its transmit port. This feature enables, for example, hot-plugging of additional Sercos devices and transmission of the telegrams to devices using other protocols.

If the last subordinate device is connected to the master, a ring topology is formed. With such a topology, the master sends data through the ring in opposite directions using both ports. This means that in a ring, as opposed to a line, the data is evaluated in two directions. This way, the ring topology provides for a higher level of availability because an interruption in a ring does not necessarily lead to an interruption of synchronization and communication as long as a device can be reached “the other way round”.

NOTE: Ethernet/IP devices must be connected to the last Sercos device of the line.
