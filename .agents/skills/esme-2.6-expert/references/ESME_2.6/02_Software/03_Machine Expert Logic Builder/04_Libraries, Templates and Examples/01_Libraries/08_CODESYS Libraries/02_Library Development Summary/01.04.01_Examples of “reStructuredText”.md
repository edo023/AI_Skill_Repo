## Examples of “reStructuredText”

> Within the scope of CODESYS libraries, two examples are provided here:
>
> * Referencing symbols to each other in comments in the [SysFile](../_downloads/5cc484451c1c76d4b2169372f9d80b9b/SysFile.library) library
> * Using images in the [SML\_Basic](../_downloads/2af502e2fe75e10751cf23b31f30553a/SML_Basic_LibDoc_Demo.library) library sources
>
> [Write the Docs](http://docs.writethedocs.org/) is where the art and science of documentation can be practiced and appreciated. There are many people who write documentation, but there is no good place to find information, ask questions, or generally be a member of a community of those who write documentation.
>
> On the Internet, there are many examples of how to use reStructuredText, Docutils, and the Sphinx toolchain. Some useful examples are as follows:
>
> * The [PDF](../../../../../../api/crossBook?lang=en-US&virtualBookName=sphinx), or [HTML](http://sphinx-doc.org).
> * The [PDF](../../../../../../api/crossBook?lang=en-US&virtualBookName=jinja2), or [HTML](http://jinja2.readthedocs.org/en/latest).
> * The [Django](https://www.djangoproject.com/) project also maintains its [Documentation](https://github.com/django/django/tree/master/docs) also in reStructuredText and publishes its [Online Help](https://docs.djangoproject.com) – a nice example of how elegantly different versions of documentation can be accessed.
> * There are also documents that deal comprehensively with mathematical or physical topics. The [PDF](../../../../../../api/crossBook?lang=en-US&virtualBookName=theoretical-physics) and [HTML](http://www.theoretical-physics.net/dev/index.html).
> * [PDF](../../../../../../api/crossBook?lang=en-US&virtualBookName=read-the-docs) and [HTML](http://read-the-docs.readthedocs.org/en/latest/).
> * There are currently two different options for generating a \*.pdf file from reStructuredText:
>
>   The book [“Music for Geeks and Nerds”](../../../../../../api/crossBook?lang=en-US&virtualBookName=music-for-geeks-and-nerds-sample) by Pedro Kroger was generated by Sphinx via the LaTeX Builder and then [converted](http://pedrokroger.net/using-sphinx-write-technical-books/) to PDF via the TeX toolchain.
>
>   In contrast, the [Online Version](../../../../../../api/crossBook?lang=en-US&virtualBookName=RLIMG_f32c7c0a6db7b442726ebb8b3d3e3d0e) of the German translation the book “Python Reference” by David Beazley was generated by Sphinx with a [rts2pdf](http://rst2pdf.ralsina.me/) extension. This extension is based on the [open-source PDF Toolkit](http://www.reportlab.com/opensource/) from ReportLab.
>
>   The extra effort of installing TeX results in an widely-accepted, high-quality typeset (for example, clean margin alignment of paragraphs, including hyphenation). On the other hand, “rst2pdf” can be used in the short term for obtaining correct PDF files, however with lower quality typeset and no hyperlinks.
