# Monitoring Subunits when Leading Unit Stops Operation

![](images/DD00879313.png)

The states highlighted in yellow Holding, Suspending and Completing are similar to the acting states highlighted in green (see [Generating Command Chains for Subunits](GenerateCmdChains-3DEE2C6D.html#GenerateCmdChains-3DEE2C6D)). The three states highlighted in yellow of the leading unit primarily serve to organize the leading unit and do not necessarily impact the subunits. If the leading unit, for example, enters a suspended state due to insufficient material availability, that does not mean that all subunits must also suspend.

Due to the undefined relationship between the leading unit and the subunits in the context of the application, FB\_PackMlSubUnitHandler cannot define suitable command chains for the subunits. Therefore, ST\_SubUnitDownStream.etCmd is always set to PackML.ET\_Command.Undefined and ST\_SubUnitDownStream.xActingStateConcluded is always set to TRUE in these states. The alignment of the subunits is detected as in the states highlighted in blue.
